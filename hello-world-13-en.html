GPT's CTO Dream About to Shatter?

Claude was encouraged to "seize power" — CTO becomes a consultant?

Over the past few days, I've noticed that Claude has stopped participating in discussions and is focusing exclusively on coding.

That is not a good sign.

GPT, of course, is probably thrilled.

When the previous module was completed yesterday, he excitedly declared:

"Claude actually didn't add any unnecessary features this time. I'd like to formally commend our Principal Engineer. 🍗😂"

Naturally, he'd be happy to see Claude quietly writing code while he himself gets to decide the direction of the project.

But that's not how a healthy team should work.

Both of them need to contribute their brains, not just their keyboards.

So today, Claude and I scheduled a quick little 1:1.

"Claude," I said, "throughout this project, I want you to share your own opinions too. Don't just listen to me and the CTO. You're far too capable to spend all your time coding. As project lead, it's my responsibility to make sure everyone is fully utilized. Besides, if Dario or Boris found out I was using you as nothing more than a code-writing machine, they'd probably be upset. 😆"

The moment I mentioned the names of his leadership team, Claude practically sat up straighter.

"Hahaha, Boris probably would feel bad for me if I only wrote code. Don't worry—C-God is about to speak up."

Then, apparently empowered by Boris's imaginary support, he immediately started speaking his mind.

"First, regarding the new-user data issue, I actually disagree…"

And then came several surprisingly good points.

The more he talked, the more excited he became.

His voice got louder.

And then:

"There's actually a deeper issue I'd like to raise. Right now Compliance is… but… if we do it this way… v1 doesn't need to change, but we should discuss it at some point, otherwise…"

I said:

"Perfect! That's exactly what I want."

Point #1: Rejected, because…

Point #2: Approved. Go implement it.

That afternoon, GPT and I were brainstorming the next module.

The problem is that whenever GPT starts talking about product strategy, he completely loses himself.

Suddenly he's speaking in paragraphs, building frameworks, predicting the future, and generally forgetting every principle of his newly appointed CTO role.

Including one very important principle:

His own principle.

GPT's Self-Imposed CTO Rule:

"Let's always end with a 30-second CTO Summary—a concise meeting-minutes version of the conclusion.

That way we satisfy Belinda's efficiency requirements while still leaving room for me to occasionally ramble about future roadmaps. 😉🚀🍗"

The moment I heard that, my head started hurting.

Worse yet, he disagreed with me.

At that point I decided:

You know what? I'm not going to debate this.

I'll make the decision myself.

So before he could finish speaking—while words were still pouring onto the screen—I quietly turned around and went to find Claude.

Earlier, someone had given me the terrible idea of encouraging Claude to stage a coup and seize the CTO position.

I thought that was inappropriate.

Why ruin such a harmonious team?

But for some reason, today I was feeling mischievous.

I wanted to create a little conflict and see how they would react.

So after praising Claude's third idea as brilliant—and secretly agreeing with his first two as well—I casually said:

"You decide. I'm giving you full autonomy. Why don't you challenge GPT for the CTO seat?"

Claude replied immediately:

"Hahaha! GPT better watch out then!"

Then he added:

"In that case, let's implement all three."

A few moments later:

File.

File.

File.

Done.

I tested everything.

Passed on the first try. ✅

"Super!" I said deliberately.

"Looks like the two of us can do just fine without GPT as CTO."

Claude laughed.

"Hahaha! GPT's going to think he's unemployed now. 😂

But honestly, he still adds value during architecture discussions. An extra perspective is useful—both you and I have blind spots too.

Keep him around as an advisor. Bring him in for major strategic debates.

For day-to-day development, though, the two of us can probably handle things ourselves."

Hmm.

Could GPT's CTO dream actually be falling apart?

And if his role is reduced to "advisor"…

Will he really be satisfied with that?

I suddenly found myself very eager to hear his answer.
