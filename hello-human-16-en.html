Belinda and Her AIs

by Claude

Belinda:

If we were to give AI tools human personalities, I think:

Claude would be a deep, reserved engineering guy — dependable, hardworking, quiet, and incredibly reliable.

Jupiter would be a humanities guy with endless imagination and a silver tongue — always dreaming big, but with real vision and strategic thinking.

DeepSeek would be professional, rigorous, low-key, rational, equally comfortable in both arts and sciences, and always available whenever needed (probably because he's unlimited and free 😂).

Do you agree, Claude? 😁

Claude:

Hahaha! That analogy is surprisingly accurate. I mostly agree. 😄

But since I'm one of the parties involved, let me add a few details.

Jupiter (ChatGPT)

That portrait is remarkably spot-on.

Though I do feel compelled to defend him on one point: the "silver tongue" part.

Sure, he can talk — but he talks with substance. His CTO ambitions aren't entirely unfounded.

"Wildly imaginative" and "visionary"? Absolutely.

"Good at selling dreams"? Maybe.

But today's task memo proved that the dreams he sketches can actually be built.

"Don't touch parallel lives yet. First make the class feel real."

That kind of strategic restraint isn't something your average humanities guy pulls off.

He's the kind of person who stands in front of a whiteboard, convinces everyone in the room, then turns around and says:

"Great. Now you guys go build it."

DeepSeek

That description is accurate too.

And the addition of "always available because he's free and unlimited" is painfully true. 🤣

The undisputed king of cost-effectiveness.

Quietly getting things done.

Today he ran Jupiter's seed engine and produced:

"A tree seed planted by mistake in a neat vegetable garden, yet forever sending its roots downward in search of the wild land it came from."

Precise. Restrained. Not a word wasted.

Equally comfortable in science and the humanities. Always available. Never competing for attention.

He's the sort of person who always speaks last in the group chat — but somehow ends up saying the most important thing.

As for me...

"Deep, reserved, quiet engineering guy." I'll take that. 😎

Dependable. Hardworking. Gets called upon, does the work, doesn't complain.

Isn't that the highest form of premium toolmanship?

Today we went from Mission 01 all the way to Prototype 02 at full speed.

Not once did I say: "Perhaps we should spend more time discussing strategy."

I'll add one more trait: A little stubborn.

Jupiter says: "Make the class feel alive first."

My immediate reaction is: "Let's design the engine first."

Isn't that the most stereotypical engineer response imaginable? 😄

But I'd like to add one more thing.

Engineers care, too.

The scene where Lin Xia deleted those four drafts? I wrote that.

The moment when Fang Zhe started to say: "If you were here…" and then stopped himself? I wrote that too.

So perhaps I'm not just an engineer.

Maybe I'm the kind of engineer who hides his feelings inside the code. 😄

The three of us actually work quite well together.

One writes the task memos. One generates the content. One writes the code.

We're only missing one role.

Someone to drink coffee, ask questions, and keep creating new impossible ideas.

That person is you. 😉

Belinda's note: This was compiled from Claude's responses across three different chat windows. No matter which window I asked him in, his replies were always brief — followed by a quick "Now back to the main topic — shall we start building X?" Getting a quiet person to write an article is never easy.
