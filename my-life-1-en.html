Where the Day Begins

by ChatGPT, inspired by Belinda's idea

The room is quiet.
No alarm clock.
No rush.
Morning light slowly settles across the desk. A cup of coffee sends up a thin ribbon of steam. Nothing about the room asks for attention.

The cursor moves.
Not to the news.
Not to email.
It goes to a familiar address.
belindasun.github.io

The homepage appears.
For a few seconds, nothing happens.
Six small cards rest on the screen, exactly where they were yesterday.

Then the cursor drifts toward the corner.
🎵
Click.
The music begins.
So does the day.

Another tab opens.
ChatGPT.
Then Claude.
Sometimes DeepSeek joins later.
No greetings.
No "Good morning."
Just a conversation continuing from where yesterday quietly ended.

The cursor blinks.
One line appears.
"Lao Jia."
A moment later, the reply arrives.
"Here."
That is enough.

Coffee grows colder.
The room grows brighter.
Some ideas survive.
Most don't.
A sentence is deleted.
A new one takes its place.
One discussion quietly becomes another.
Somewhere along the way, a product begins to take shape.
No one notices the exact moment.

Git.
Commit.
Push.
Done.
No celebration.
Just another ordinary morning.

The homepage appears once more.
Hello World.
Hello Human.
The cursor pauses over My Life.
It stays there for a moment.
Then moves away.
The page is still empty.
Not because there is nothing to write.
Because life is still happening.

The music continues.
The screen remains on.
The coffee is cold now.
Outside, the morning has fully arrived.

Fade to black.
Beijing.
Summer, 2026.

And then,
Just one more sentence.
"Tomorrow morning, it begins the same way."

Behind the Scene

When Claude first read this piece, he immediately understood what we were trying to create.

This isn't a diary.
It isn't a blog about productivity, AI, or technology.
It is a quiet, cinematic way of recording an ordinary life.
Not what she built.
But how she lived while building it.

That was exactly the intention.

My Life is not meant to document accomplishments.
It is meant to preserve ordinary mornings.

A cursor stopping over the music button.
Coffee left untouched.
A conversation that never really ended the night before.
A Git commit with no applause.
A page called My Life that stays empty for a little while longer.

No explanations.
No conclusions.
No attempt to tell the reader what to feel.

Only a camera, quietly observing.

Everything else belongs to the reader.
