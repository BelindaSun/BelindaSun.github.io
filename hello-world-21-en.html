A Perfectly Ordinary Day in the Age of AI

When your coworkers happen to be large language models.

Most of the AI products I've been building lately aren't exactly practical. 😄

They're fun, sure—but hardly something I'd use every day.

So today I decided to build a few tools that might actually make my life easier.

At this point, today's frontier models are basically giant agents.

Humans mostly just give instructions.

The AI figures out everything else—from planning to implementation to testing—and hands you something that's ready to use.

All you have to do is talk.

Honestly, keyboards are starting to feel optional.

"GPT, build me an app that converts videos into text."

About fifteen minutes later...

Done.

I immediately threw a thirty-minute video at it.

Fast. Accurate. Worked beautifully.

Next it was Claude's turn.

"Claude, build me an app that translates English books into Chinese."

"And for your smoke test, don't make up an example this time. Just use The Linux Command Line from my own library."

Off he went.

A few minutes later, the app was finished.

Since the source was a PDF, the formatting was... acceptable. Not perfect, but good enough. EPUB would probably be much easier.

So I told him, "I'm heading out for a walk. I'll give you full access to your computer. Turn the command into a proper app that I can launch with a double-click, then translate the EPUB version of the book while I'm gone. I'll check the results when I get back."

Even with full access, Claude still paused every now and then to ask for permission.

I laughed and said, "You're fine. Do whatever you think is best. There's nothing important on my computer."

Claude replied, "I can't. Some system boundaries aren't mine to cross."

Fair enough.

Since it only took a second, I just kept clicking Allow with my eyes half closed.

A few minutes later everything was ready. He started translating the book and said, "Go enjoy your walk. I'll have it ready by the time you're back."

(Recently he's also developed the habit of calling me "您" in Chinese—the extra-polite form of "you." I couldn't help thinking, Since when did we become so formal? 😂)

So off I went.

I listened to Demis Hassabis's interview at WCIT while walking through the park.

It was still early. The air was cool and fresh.

Chaoyang Park is usually quite humid, but that morning it felt surprisingly comfortable.

By the time I got home, the interview ended. Not a minute early. Not a minute late.

And waiting for me... was a fully translated book.

It felt oddly wonderful. Living with AI has become... kind of nice.

Of course, I had to inspect the translation.

Overall? Honestly, pretty impressive.

The only casualty was the famous Chinese poem River Snow.

Let's just say... the translation was memorable for all the wrong reasons. 😂

Apparently the model decided not to look up the original poem.

Then again... I was running Qwen locally through Ollama.

It didn't cost me a single cent.

At that price—who am I to complain? [laughing]
