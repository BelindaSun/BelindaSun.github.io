How Beautiful Summer Nights Are

The moon tonight is so delicately curved, hanging low in the western sky.

And the stars… so few. I laugh softly to myself as I tilt my head upward, searching carefully before finally spotting a handful scattered thinly across the distant heavens, each one hanging there in quiet solitude. This is simply what city nights are like. Even in summer, I no longer expect the endless oceans of starlight I once dreamed of. A few stars are enough now.

And yet, I know that somewhere above, the Cowherd and the Weaver Girl are still gazing toward one another across the vast Silver River that forever divides them. Thousands of years have passed, and still they wait. Still they long. Still they love. On summer nights, such love feels especially beautiful. I lift my eyes toward the sky. Though the city lights blur my vision, I know they are there. My heart can feel them. And perhaps what the heart senses reaches far farther, and lasts far longer, than anything the eyes can ever truly see.

Down below, within the neighborhood, people stroll leisurely through the evening. Dogs roll happily in the grass. Flowers seem almost to smile in the night breeze, and waves of fragrance drift softly through the air. That very morning, when I opened the window, I suddenly noticed a parasol tree blooming quietly in the distance. Graceful and understated, it reminded me of a woman untouched by worldly noise — serene, elegant, her skirts floating lightly in the wind. At that moment I could almost smell the faint lavender sweetness of her blossoms. And now, in the cool night breeze, I am certain part of this fragrance comes from that dancing fairy of the parasol tree herself.

The streetlights have come on, glowing like scattered stars, drawing my thoughts farther and farther away. The wind brushes against my face — cool, gentle, wonderfully rare for a summer night. Tree shadows sway softly in the darkness. Grass releases its faint green scent. Everything feels impossibly beautiful.

And yet… Why are there no fireflies? I lower my head and search among the grass and beneath the trees, but not a single one appears.

The night I remember most vividly was in Malaysia. The moon hung halfway across the sky. Water drifted beneath moonlight. The boat floated gently across the water. We sat upon the boat while fireflies danced all around us — so close that with one quick reach of the hand, you could catch one. “I got one! I got one!” The joy of that moment still glimmers somewhere inside me.

But tonight, where have all the fireflies gone?

Perhaps the streetlights are too bright. Perhaps the moonlight is too beautiful. Perhaps the night itself is too intoxicating. Perhaps even the fireflies have become drunk on summer.

Nearby, children are reciting a nursery rhyme:

“The curved moon is a little boat,
A little boat with pointed ends.
I sit inside the little boat,
Seeing only twinkling stars
And endless blue skies…”

And so I sit there quietly, watching those happy children. I look up at the night sky. I listen carefully to the whispers of flowers. I lower my head and breathe in the scent of grass.

And suddenly, like the little star in my daughter’s textbook, I cannot help but exclaim with simple happiness:

Ah… How beautiful summer nights are.

— June, 2006
