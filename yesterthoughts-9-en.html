LIUDODO's Legendary One-Liners

After nearly an hour in the swimming pool, LIUDODO and I climbed out, rinsed off, and decided to rest for a while by the water.

LIUDODO's father never got into the pool. He stayed stretched out on a lounge chair nearby surfing the internet. But he's irrelevant to this story, so we shall not dwell on him. In cinematic terms, he existed merely as background scenery — at best a prop, at worst an unpaid extra. 😆

It was already past nine in the evening. The night breeze drifted gently across our skin. The moonlight was luminous and soft. Wrapped in towels, we felt perfectly comfortable — neither warm nor cold.

LIUDODO and I lay side by side on lounge chairs chatting lazily. Above us hung dense tropical leaves swaying gracefully in the wind. Beneath their woven canopy, pieces of white cloud floated slowly across the sky. The leaves rustled softly. Moonlight spilled like silk across the pool. The breeze brushed ripples over the water. In the distance, scattered streetlights glowed quietly like tiny stars, decorating and guarding the peaceful scene without disturbing its stillness in the slightest.

Shadows of trees. Faint fragrance of grass. At that moment, everything in the world felt almost impossibly balanced — neither too intense nor too plain. Everything was nearly perfect. Including the mood.

I sighed happily: “What a beautiful night. If only there were some gentle music right now — maybe a serenade or a light waltz — then it would truly be perfect.”

I turned to look at LIUDODO. She had narrowed her eyes dreamily, completely immersed in the atmosphere. Her face carried that unmistakable expression of enchantment — soft, distant, emotional, almost yearning.

In that instant, I felt secretly delighted that I had somehow given birth to a daughter just like myself: someone easily moved by beauty, someone capable of being emotionally wrecked by moonlight and scenery.

Then LIUDODO slowly murmured:

“What a beautiful night. If only someone brought me a giant chocolate or cream cake right now… then it would truly be perfect.”
