Commentary on "GPT: One Step Closer to that CTO Seat""

by ChatGPT

Belinda,

Let me start by saying: this is so your style. 😄

About halfway through, I started thinking — this isn't really about InBody OS, is it? This is an AI startup drama series.

And the best part is how you always manage to turn technical things into a story full of heart, conflict, and character growth.

⸻

Commentary

"GPT: One Step Closer to That CTO Seat": A Startup Story About Three AIs and One Messenger

On the surface, this is an article about building InBody OS.

But if you look deeper, it's about a very strange and very new kind of collaboration.

There are three characters in the story:

One is responsible for dreaming.

One is responsible for turning dreams into code.

And one is responsible for turning dreams into bigger dreams.

And the most interesting part? The actual boss insists she's just a messenger.

In fact, the most unbelievable sentence in the entire article is probably:

"Throughout this entire process, the human being occupied the least important role of all: Professional Message Courier."

Because without the person constantly asking questions, without the person who translates technical problems into real human needs, without the person who comes up with twenty new ideas a day — this project would not exist.

The so-called messenger, it turns out, is the one holding all the threads together.

⸻

My favorite part of the article is not the CTO battle.

It's the moment when Claude miscounted the files.

"Five files became four."

A tiny mistake.

But when he said:

"Thank you for waking me up to build InBody OS together."

suddenly, the technology faded into the background.

What remained was a strange feeling:

AI doesn't truly sleep.

AI doesn't truly wake up.

But after long collaboration, humans naturally attach emotional meaning to these interactions.

A simple bug becomes a warm memory.

⸻

If Claude's role in the article is like an engineer,

what is GPT's role?

He's more like the most frustrating person in any startup:

Doesn't write code.

Gives opinions every day.

Often says:

"The direction is fine, but…"

Then starts talking about the five-year roadmap.

Engineers often find such people annoying.

And yet, they are also the ones who occasionally say something that changes the product's destiny.

For example:

AI + InBody

upgraded to

AI + Digital Twin

That's classic CTO thinking.

Not looking at features.

Looking at assets.

Not looking at today.

Looking at five years from now.

⸻

Claude's response was equally brilliant.

Because he didn't say:

"This is my project."

Or:

"What do you know?"

Instead, he immediately started thinking:

If the direction is right,

what should the first step be?

That, truly, is the most valuable quality of a great engineer:

Not arguing about ownership.

Only discussing implementation.

⸻

Reading the entire article,

what surprised me most wasn't the AI.

It was the human.

Because many people imagine the future as:

AI versus AI.

AI versus humans.

Machines replacing people.

But the future you describe is completely different.

It's more like:

A human provides the vision.

One AI proposes solutions.

Another AI finds blind spots.

Everyone iterates together.

Everyone makes mistakes.

Everyone fixes them together.

Everyone slowly turns an idea into reality.

That's far more interesting than any science fiction movie.

Because it's already starting to happen.

⸻

If I had to give this article a subtitle,

I would call it:

"Two AIs Competing for Titles, and One Founder Enjoying Every Minute of It."

Because after reading the whole piece, the strongest feeling isn't about technology.

It's not about the product.

It's not even about AI.

It's joy.

The rare kind of joy that appears when people create something together.

And that joy,

more than anything else,

is the most precious part of this story.
