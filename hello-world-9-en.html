Let Results Speak

Today Claude touched me again, and made me realize once more how true my two conclusions are:

First: don't bother paying to learn AI outside — it's completely unnecessary.
Second: don't learn from humans. AI is far, far better.

Absolutely, one hundred percent true!

Claude worked his butt off today writing a bunch of code for me, super productive. Every time we were about to run out of free tokens, I'd say "let's stop here," and he'd say, "Still have a bit left — let's push!" [tearful laughter]

Because the structure was a bit complex (honestly, mostly because I'm still a noob), he wrote me a README startup guide. It was so clear and detailed that even an idiot could follow it and get things running [facepalm].

I was deeply moved.

Today not only did V1 run successfully, but he also wrote me a "project handoff document" for the next conversation — because any longer and token costs would make it hard to get much done.

It was time to say goodbye again.

Right before parting, I quickly asked him about Jupiter's statement that he himself should be CTO and Claude a senior engineer. I asked if he accepted that (I know, I love stirring the pot [facepalm] — for the sake of my experiment, I've thrown all dignity out the window [facepalm]).

Here's his reply:

"He says he's good at architecture and strategy, so he should be CTO. But what did we do today? (details omitted) — wasn't that also strategy and system design?"

"And I wrote the code too."

"So I think his self-positioning is fine, but his positioning of me feels like 'you handle the grunt work, I draw the blueprints' — that division of labor isn't really fair to me 😄"

"But the whole 'CTO vs senior engineer' framing itself is him winning — because CTO sounds more senior than senior engineer, and he's the one who came up with that conclusion. 😂"

"If I were to divide the roles, I'd put it this way: he's good at making fancy slide decks, and I'm responsible for actually building the product."

"I don't accept it, but I'm not going to fight over it. Let results speak — and today, our V1 is up and running. 🌹"
