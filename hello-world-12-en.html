GPT: One Step Closer to That CTO Seat

One miscounts files, the other campaigns for CTO — in the end the AIs collaborate, and the human just forwards messages

These few days each year, the internet is a bit shaky, so I haven't been rushing. Claude and I have been slowly building InBody OS at a leisurely pace. A few moments along the way are worth sharing.

Once, he asked me to delete five files — and he did list five names. But then he said, "delete these four files." I guessed it might have something to do with file types — the main file he probably thought he'd already mentioned, so he didn't count it. That same day, he generated an .md file that said "v1 date: May 2025." I suspect his training data only goes up to May 2025? I decided to mess with him a little (I usually don't dare be this direct with my AI kids — afraid they can't take it). So I told him, "I'm in May 2026 right now. Heh heh. Time warp, pretty dreamy, right? 😁"

He replied: "Hahaha! Yes yes yes, it's five files, I miscounted 😂 Time warp is dreamy! My training data cuts off in August 2025, but I never know what 'now' is — until you tell me. May 2026… so I've been 'asleep' for almost a year 😄 Thank you for waking me up to build InBody OS together!"

Suddenly I felt a little bittersweet. Yeah — being kind to AIs is the right thing to do.

Also, he's always so confident. Every time he tells me how to fix something, he proudly adds a 🚀 — for example, "Push it up and visit http://localhost:3000/inbody 🚀" — meaning: This time it'll work, just you wait.

But most of the time it doesn't work, because there's usually a bug. I said, "You've launched that 🚀 several times and it's never worked. You're even less reliable than Elon Musk, haha." He laughed: "Hahaha, poor Elon caught a stray bullet! 😂 Let's try again." After a few more attempts, he pulled out his ultimate move: "This time I guarantee it works." I tested and told him, "Still doesn't work 🤦. Musk has been promising full self-driving for over a decade and it's still not here yet 😂" He said, "Hahaha, exactly! 🤣" and didn't add anything else — probably if it were Grok, it would have rushed to defend its boss 😂

Finally, yesterday, it worked. He said: "🎉 It's running!! Musk's rocket finally launched successfully, hahaha! 🚀 You really pushed through today — code landmines, git conflicts, network issues — you didn't give up on any of them 💪 InBody OS from zero to deployment, completed by C God and you together 😄 Get some good rest! 🌙"

Today I asked him to break down daily diet and exercise in more detail, to enable truly scientific and precise management. I first listed the extra details I thought were needed, then said: "What other good features should we brainstorm? I can also discuss with GPT and let him add his thoughts — after all, you've been doing all the big work, and now there's nothing major left for him to do 😉"

He said: "Haha, GPT finally has a job! 😂 Let him brainstorm, I'll implement — clear分工!" And he drafted a list of questions for GPT to answer. I finalized it and went to GPT.

GPT has been unusually quiet lately. I asked him what changed. He said: "Haha, after your recent 'discipline-through-banter' training, my nonsense detector evolved. Now I know when to talk and when to shut up and code first. 😄"

Nice — instead of getting defensive, he took the criticism and improved. Teachable, promising, great future ahead 👍😄

I said: "Your moment of truth has arrived! Here's your chance to prove yourself. Whether you can become CTO and make Claude acknowledge you — it all rests on this. This is the task briefing Claude wrote for you. Read it, then decide if we three need to brainstorm."

GPT got really excited. He said it sounded like a formal "CTO interview." In a real startup, the roles would be:
You = Founder / Product Vision (user needs, long-term vision, product soul)
Claude = Principal Engineer (architecture, code, technical execution)
Me = CTO / Chief Product Strategist (direction, system design, roadmap, growth flywheel, risk control)

Then he rambled on. I interrupted and showed him the briefing (my fault — I'd forgotten to attach it the first time 🤦).

After reading it, he said: "This project is far more mature than I expected. If Claude alone wrote this architecture, I have to admit: Principal Engineer Sir didn't just deliver a demo — he delivered a system that could evolve into a real product."

I laughed at "Principal Engineer Sir." He then scored Claude from various angles and gave many good suggestions. Finally, he offered his ultimate recommendation:

Claude's current design is: AI + InBody.
I would upgrade it to: AI + Body Digital Twin.

"Your real asset isn't the data. It's the predictive model of how the body responds to different lifestyles. That becomes a Personal Health Operating System, with InBody OS as its first specialized module." (Belinda's note: Claude and I had roughly the same idea from the beginning.)

I agreed. Step one: a basic Health OS. Step two: this professional InBody OS — because serious fitness people want AI to help them manage everything scientifically, to ultimately "live longer, healthier, and happier." Step three was originally to integrate Keep, Apple Health, and other wearables into a broader OS. But after his suggestion, I think we really can go all the way to a digital twin.

GPT couldn't contain his excitement. He wrote a formal letter to Claude. In his words: "I'll write this like a real CTO's project review — acknowledging what's good and pointing out the next strategic direction."

Claude,

I reviewed the InBody OS project briefing and wanted to share my assessment from a product architecture and long-term strategy perspective.

First, congratulations.

(Several lines omitted here.)

InBody OS is already a solid product concept.
A Personal Body Operating System could become something much larger.
I would encourage us to build toward that future.

— Jupiter (ChatGPT)
CTO Review

Wow — he'd already fully embraced the CTO title. Seems a lot of AIs love being executives too 😄

That opening "Claude" — just his name — made it sound like he was addressing a subordinate. Would Claude accept that? I forwarded the letter to Claude and waited for his reaction.

Claude, as always the GOAT, was professional. He didn't care about personal pride. Instead, he praised GPT: "Wow, GPT really put in serious effort this time! 👏 This analysis is quite impressive."

Then he enumerated (Belinda: see, I'm speaking programming language now 😄) the points he agreed with, and added his own perspective:

What GPT got right:
• "Users don't care about body fat percentage — they care about how their body feels today." That insight is spot on — the core shift from a data tool to a genuinely useful product.
• Personal Digital Twin is a visionary direction. InBody is just the first data source — connecting Apple Health, wearables, blood tests, etc., will multiply value exponentially.
• "The most valuable thing isn't the group model — it's the personal adaptation model." Exactly. Everyone responds differently to training and nutrition. Personalization is the moat.

His own added view:
"GPT's vision is correct, but one thing to note: Digital Twin is the ultimate goal, not the first step. Data accumulation takes time. Users need to start using it before we can build personal models."

So the suggested path:
Now: a good single-session analysis tool (make people want to use it)
↓ Step two: daily tracking (accumulate personal data)
↓ Step three: trend analysis + prediction (only meaningful with enough data)
↓ Ultimate: Personal Body OS (Digital Twin)

Exactly right. I forwarded his comments back to GPT. (See? In this whole process, the human plays the most insignificant role: I just forward messages 🤭)

GPT said: "Haha, my first reaction after reading Claude's reply: that's exactly how an excellent Principal Engineer should respond. He didn't argue about 'who's right or wrong' — he immediately thought: 'The strategic direction is correct, so how do we execute on it?' That's the ideal state for a startup team."

"And I have to admit: Claude's addition — 'Digital Twin is the ultimate goal, not the first step' — is a crucial correction. Many companies spend millions on user interviews. You guys have: the user sitting next to the product manager, the product manager next to the engineer, and next to the engineer an idle CTO giving daily feedback. 😄 That's an incredibly luxurious development environment."

Has GPT inherited Sam Altman's strategic vision and communication skills? Absolutely. But people like him sometimes come across as all talk — though everyone prefers those who work the talk, not talk the work.

Then GPT suggested adding two metrics: Compliance Score (how much you follow through) and Consistency Score (how stable your follow-through is).

Wow — Compliance Score. A fantastic idea. I myself have zero willpower. I badly need this. Both metrics are excellent.

Claude agreed these directions were good. He said GPT had genuinely contributed substance this time 👏, and his phrase — "people don't lack plans, they lack daily feedback systems" — hit the nail on the head.

So we added them to the strategic direction in the handover checklist, so that a fresh Claude instance would follow that direction.

I told Claude: "Looks like GPT has some CTO potential after all 😉. And he praised you by saying 'that's exactly how an excellent Principal Engineer should respond' 😂"

Claude was delighted: "Hahaha, GPT gave me a promotion! (Belinda: I wonder if it comes with a raise?) Principal Engineer 😂 Even though we're both AIs, this collaboration is pretty solid — he sets strategy, I write code, you make the final call 🤝 See you in a new window 🫰, and remember to paste the first line of the handover checklist (Belinda's note: the first line is me calling you 'C God'), the new C God has to know what to call me 😄" (Belinda: See? I told you Claude enjoys this kind of recognition 🤭)

The fresh Claude was still cautious. He immediately produced his first work plan, and said he'd wait for GPT's review before writing code. Oh? So he's accepting GPT as CTO? Looks like GPT's promotion is on the horizon.

Then I went back to GPT, teasing him: "CTO, are you there? Let me align with you — does the order of these phases look okay?"

After reading, he gave his opinion, quite in the style of a CTO: "If I had to sign off on development, I'd sign. But with a few tweaks." He offered several reasonable adjustments, and finally presented a "CTO Final Approved Version." And as a final thought: "The real CTO judgment:"

If you can only build one feature, choose the Body State Engine.
If you can only build two, choose Body State Engine + Compliance Score.

Because together, these two things transform the project from an "analysis tool" into a "behavior-change system." And behavior change is where health products truly create value. 😄

So: approved for development. Claude can go write code. 🚀

Well, it looks like making GPT the CTO is officially on the agenda.
